## Introduction

Having examined in the preceding documents the virtue of knowledge, the fiqh of priorities, the instrumental and purposive sciences, gradual progression, etiquette, the balance of specialisation and breadth, and authorship — the time has come to present an integrated practical model that brings these elements together in an applicable plan.

This model is not a rigid prescription for everyone. Rather, it is a flexible framework that each student may adapt according to their circumstances, capacities, and goals. Al-Ghazali said: "Knowledge is not a tool cast into the heart of whomever He wills — it is the fruit of effort and exertion."

## The Practical Model

### Year One — Foundation

| Quarter | Topics                | Suggested Books                               | Weekly Hours |
| ------- | --------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| First   | Creed and Tawhid      | "Al-Usul al-Thalathah", "Al-Qawa'id al-Arba'" | 10 hours     |
| Second  | Worship Jurisprudence | Concise fiqh text (according to school)       | 10 hours     |
| Third   | Prophetic Biography   | "Mukhtasar Sirat al-Rasul"                    | 8 hours      |
| Fourth  | Qur'anic Sciences     | Introduction to Qur'anic sciences             | 8 hours      |

**Guidance:** This is the planting year. Do not rush the harvest. The goal is building the cognitive and creedal foundation.

### Year Two — Linguistic and Foundational Building

| Quarter | Topics               | Suggested Books                               | Weekly Hours |
| ------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| First   | Grammar (1)          | Al-Ajurrumiyyah                               | 10 hours     |
| Second  | Grammar (2)          | Commentary on al-Ajurrumiyyah or Qatr al-Nada | 10 hours     |
| Third   | Morphology           | Lamiat al-Af'al or Sarf text                  | 8 hours      |
| Fourth  | Hadith & Terminology | Al-Arba'un al-Nawawiyyah + Nukhbat al-Fikar   | 8 hours      |

**Guidance:** This is the year of instrumental sciences. Focus on language. Do not rush into juristic details before mastering the tools of understanding.

### Year Three — Expansion

| Quarter | Topics           | Suggested Books                         | Weekly Hours |
| ------- | ---------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------ |
| First   | Exegesis         | Juz Amma from al-Sa'di or Ibn Kathir    | 10 hours     |
| Second  | Comparative Fiqh | Simple comparison of schools in worship | 10 hours     |
| Third   | Usul al-Fiqh     | Al-Waraqat                              | 8 hours      |
| Fourth  | Islamic History  | Outline of Islamic history              | 8 hours      |

**Guidance:** Begin extensive reading. Time to move from universals to particulars, from core texts to commentaries.

### Year Four — Specialisation and Distinction

| Quarter | Topics               | Specialisation (choose one track)                    |
| ------- | -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| First   | Track 1: Exegesis    | Deep study of Tafsir Ibn Kathir or al-Qurtubi        |
| Second  | Track 2: Hadith      | Fath al-Bari or Umdat al-Qari                        |
| Third   | Track 3: Fiqh        | Al-Mughni or al-Muhalla                              |
| Fourth  | Scholarly Production | Writing a short article or paper in the chosen field |

**Guidance:** This year is open according to the student's inclination. Choose one track and go deep. Begin scholarly production, even a short article.

<div class="callout callout-warning">

**Important Note:** This timetable is approximate, not mandatory. Some students may need double this time; others may accomplish more in less. What matters is continuity, not speed. "A little that continues is better than much that is interrupted."

</div>

## Daily Operating Principles

Beyond the yearly plan, daily principles must be observed:

1. **Daily Qur'an portion:** Never neglect a daily portion of Qur'an recitation, however short.
2. **Regular daily reading:** Allocate at least one hour daily to consistent reading.
3. **Repetition and review:** Do not move from a topic until you have mastered it. "Repetition of the lesson solidifies the foundation."
4. **Daily notation:** Write down what you learn, even a brief summary. Recorded knowledge is not lost.
5. **Dialogue and discussion:** Discuss what you learn with peers. Discussion consolidates understanding.
6. **Hour of reflection:** Set aside time for contemplation, not merely mechanical reading.

## Aids to Continuity

Continuity is the greatest challenge. Aids include:

| Aid                     | Practical Application                       |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Righteous company       | Choose serious fellow students              |
| Daily reading           | Even 15 minutes — consistency over quantity |
| Teaching                | Teach others what you learn to consolidate  |
| Varied reading          | Alternate between disciplines for freshness |
| Regular teacher contact | Do not dispense with a teacher entirely     |
| Biographical reading    | Lives of earlier scholars inspire resolve   |

## Signs of Progress

How does the student know they are advancing?

1. **Increased understanding of sacred texts:** You read a verse or hadith and grasp meanings you previously missed.
2. **Ability to distinguish between opinions:** You can tell strong from weak positions.
3. **Less haste in issuing opinions:** You pause before answering and say "Allah knows best" with confidence.
4. **Increased reverence for Allah:** ﴾Only those of His servants with knowledge fear Allah﴿ — real knowledge increases awe, not boldness.
5. **Greater humility and gratitude:** The more you know, the more aware you become of your ignorance.

## Summary

This practical model condenses what would fill dozens of documents into a brief, applicable plan. But the most important reminder: **Knowledge is a lifelong journey, not a degree earned in four years.**

The knowledge collection "Fiqh of Priorities in Seeking Knowledge" aims to chart the path for the student, providing a compass in the sea of learning. It comprises eight documents spanning from foundation to synthesis, offered in the hope of aiding every student — whether at the beginning of their path or somewhere along the way.

> Learning in youth is like engraving on stone — its trace remains as long as life lasts.

﴾Rabbana atina min ladunka rahmatan wa hayyi lana min amrina rashada﴿ [Al-Kahf: 10].
